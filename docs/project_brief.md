This document describes the highest level project briefing and essential timeline and outputs.

## Generic Briefing
The project is to build 100 LLM personas modelling one group of humans and ask them a few single-select survey questions to inform a consequential decision. Your goal is to come up with different methods of creating / modelling personas using LLMs, so that they produce results that capture the human group’s opinions as much as possible, and present your methods and findings to the team, as if they are senior decision-makers.

## My chosen direction and high-level plan

I will be modelling stances on daylight savings time for 100 humans living in new york city. Daylight savings is an important issue of public opinion and has throughout history entered into public debate. There is reason to believe that LLMs have sufficient training data on the topic (given it's recurrent relevance) to believe that they act as a strong domestic proxy or 'silica' replacement on the issue for real human thought. It is also a political issue that has relevance for many areas: including leisure business, commuting and working hours. It also appeals to a very human division in opinion. Many people want there to be more sunlight time in the afternoon all year long, whereas others would prefer having more light in the morning. There's many reasons and personal perspectives that place people on this spectrum, as well as a body of historical and scientific studies that may override these personal concerns, one way or the other, making this an interesting study into human behavior and thought, feeding into multiple persuasive appeals and individual positionality across personas in society: new york being a suitable diverse place for this to occur.

I want to seed personas primarily from census data on new york city, and sample from occupation, age (only voting population), sex, race, industry, education level (plus family circumstances if we can), following from a FlockVote paper (2025). Personas may also be seeded from opinion posts and online discussion on daylight savings which I'll scrap from X. It may be that a certain proportion of personas start as opinionated by seeding from this existing body of opinionated data, while the rest of the population is there to be persuaded or even remain ambivilent. This way we have a realistic population and a slider that can seed in pressure from opinionated actors.

The key outcome of the design must be that we can query a set of survey questions to answer social science questions on the topic of interest and have these be a reasonable (not necessarily perfect) proxy of what the actual new york population thinks. We should have some level of verification / robustness checks so that we know they roughly map onto reality, but we should be careful not to get too lost in this ambitious goal in the face of delivering a workable solution.
